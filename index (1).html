Act I
Prologue

Our goal was to find the similar traits of Shakespear works to find categories we can assign these to.

Our object was

Set corpus methodology

Set sample data

Set classification types

Analyze the sentiment graph and the blob graph

And we already knew

Shakespeare works contain rich emotional and relational patterns, sentiment analysis can measure emotional flow in text.

Shakespear's works can be roughly divided by tragedy and comedy, and there is a clear distinction.

Each Shakespeare type must include viewer-focused labels for streaming audiences.

Which lead us to analyze Shakespear’s the great 4 tragedies and comedy to get a grip of what we are dealing with.

The project searches for recurring traits across Shakespeare?s works, then turns those patterns into viewer-friendly category labels.

Act II
Method

We used Gephi lite and voyant tool to turn emotional flow into something we can measure. We have used overall sentiment to determine the tone, Gehphi lite tool to determine how close the protagonist is to the heroine, used pleasure & pain graph to measure how dramatic it is, etc.

  • Main character centric vs Character sharing spotlight
  • Romantic-centric vs not romantic-centric (히로인과의 선이 얼마나 굵은가)
  • 소수극 vs 다수극
  • Happy ending vs Sad ending vs hopeful ending (끝이 올라감 vs 끝이 내려감 vs 끝이 내려가다 올라감)
  • Dramatic vs Slow burn (sentimental 굴곡에 의해)
  • Big feelings vs Serene feelings (pleasure 과 pain이 교차하는 그 넓이가 얼마나 큰지에 따라 분류)
  • Character-Driven Lead Stories / Ensemble Character Stories
  • Romance-Forward Series / Romance-Light Stories
  • Intimate Cast Dramas / Epic Ensemble Sagas
  • Feel-Good Endings / Tragic Descents / Bittersweet Recoveries
  • High-Intensity Dramas / Slow-Burn Journeys
  • Emotionally Explosive Stories / Calm Atmospheric Stories
Character FocusMain character centric vs. shared spotlight.
RomanceRomance-forward vs. romance-light relationships.
Cast ScaleIntimate cast dramas vs. epic ensemble sagas.
Ending ShapeHappy, sad, hopeful, tragic, or bittersweet arcs.
Dramatic MotionSlow-burn journeys vs. high-intensity dramas.
Feeling RangeCalm atmosphere vs. emotionally explosive stories.

These are the attributes that we assigned on every story to find out what kind of traits does the work have.

And then, we grouped similar groups together to form a category for types.

We first analyzed mostly known works and then found out we can analyze traits by the standard we stated up there. And then we started to analyze every work of Shakespear that we can get hands of. The limitaion though is that because we didn’t know how to measure how “thick” the line should be with the romantic interest, etc, so we have to lean into intuition. We discussed how much thickness should be considered ‘enough’, but I wish we could have done a better job on it.

Act V
Reflection and Conclusion

The 9 categories would cover most of the genre that is in our lives, as it has affected our life tremendously in the past. The reason why it is so significant is because there are a lot of combination of these 6 traits, yet there are patterns that are frequent. For example, Hamlet and Macbath share a lot of characteristics of recent media, like game of thrones.

The limitation would obviously be, that there might be another feature that these things might have. For example, we didn’t utilized it much but what if we used something that indicates horror? We didn’t measure horror because we couldn’t, but without it it might be incomplete. Though, we shaped a ground to stood on, and with this structure, we can always add another data and analyze it to add to the characteristics.
